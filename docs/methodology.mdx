HLG uses **Relative Human Action Efficiency (HLG-RHAE, pronounced "ray")** to score AI systems. HLG-RHAE measures per-level action efficiency compared to a human (or, in v0.1, optimal) baseline, weighted across all 33 scoring levels (tutorial level 0 is excluded from official scoring).

## What Gets Measured

AI is scored on two criteria:

- **Completion** — How many levels did the AI solve?
- **Efficiency** — How many actions did the AI take compared to the baseline?

## What Counts as an Action

An action is a single `step()` call that submits a move (`u`, `d`, `l`, `r`, or `s`). Internal operations that do not alter the environment (tool calls, reasoning steps, retries inside the model) are not counted.

## Human Baseline

In v0.1 we use the **optimal solution length** for each level as the baseline `h_l`, taken from `verify_solutions.py`. This is a conservative proxy: real first-contact human players are expected to use materially more than `h_l` actions because they must explore before planning.

In v0.2, we will replace `h_l` with the **upper-median best human action count** measured in a controlled study (10 first-contact participants per level, no level-specific instructions, 20-minute soft cutoff).

<Note>
  Because optimal length is *strictly less than or equal to* the human baseline,
  HLG-RHAE under v0.1 yields *lower* numbers than it will under v0.2. This is
  intentional and explicitly disclosed in Section 5 of the paper.
</Note>

## How Scoring Works

### Per-Level Scoring

For each level the AI completes:

```
level_score = min(1.15, baseline_actions / ai_actions) ** 2
```

Worked examples:

- Baseline 10 actions, AI takes 10 → level score = `1.0` (100%)
- Baseline 10 actions, AI takes 20 → level score = `0.25` (25%)
- Baseline 10 actions, AI takes 100 → level score = `0.01` (1%)

### Per-Level Score Cap

The maximum score per level is capped at **1.15× baseline**. If the AI discovers a shortcut and completes a level faster than the baseline, it can receive at most 1.15. This stops a single anomalous level from disproportionately inflating the total score.

### Per-Environment Aggregation

The total HLG-RHAE is the linearly-weighted average of per-level scores, with weight `w_l = l` (the 1-indexed level number). This underweights tutorial-like early levels and overweights later levels where mastery must be demonstrated.

The maximum HLG-RHAE is also capped by the **weighted fraction of levels completed**. To reach 100% the AI must complete all 33 scoring levels, including the final ones.

Example: an AI that solves only levels 1–15 has a max possible HLG-RHAE of:

```
sum(1..15) / sum(1..33) = 120 / 561 ≈ 21.4%
```

No matter how efficiently it played levels 1–15, its HLG-RHAE cannot exceed 21.4%.

### Total Score

HLG-RHAE is a single number in `[0, 1]`, reported on the leaderboard as a percentage.

## Score Interpretation

| Score   | Interpretation                                                                            |
| ------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 100%    | AI solves all 33 levels matching or surpassing the baseline efficiency on each.            |
| 50–99%  | Strong performance: most levels solved with reasonable efficiency.                         |
| 1–49%   | Mixed performance: some levels solved but efficiency materially below baseline.            |
| 0%      | AI never completes a level, or every level it completes scores 0 due to weights/cap.      |
